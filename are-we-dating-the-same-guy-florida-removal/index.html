    Florida Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Florida provides strong defamation protections with some unique features that benefit AWDTSG victims. The FTC's Bureau of Consumer Protection provides additional resources for consumers facing online fraud and privacy violations.

    Florida Statute § 770.01 through § 770.09 governs defamation in the state. Florida requires that before filing a civil defamation action, the plaintiff must give the publisher five days' written notice specifying the defamatory statements. This pre-suit notice requirement under § 770.01 actually works in your favor for removal — it creates a formal legal demand that often prompts immediate takedown without litigation.

    Florida recognizes defamation per se for false statements that:

  • Impute a criminal offense
  • Impute an infectious or loathsome disease
  • Tend to injure someone in their trade or profession
  • Impute unchastity or sexual misconduct

    • Under per se treatment, damages are presumed. AWDTSG posts routinely contain exactly these types of false accusations. According to Pew Research Center, 41% of Americans have personally experienced some form of online harassment.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Florida is two years under Florida Statute § 95.11(4)(g). Florida courts have recognized that online republication can constitute new publication for limitations purposes.

    Florida Statute § 836.04 and § 836.05 address criminal defamation (written and spoken), providing additional legal leverage. While criminal prosecution is uncommon, the statutes' existence strengthens removal demands. Florida Statute § 784.048 covers cyberstalking, and its broad definition can encompass coordinated AWDTSG harassment campaigns. Florida's cyberstalking law is among the most comprehensive in the nation.

    Florida's right of publicity (F.S. § 540.08) protects against unauthorized commercial use of your name and likeness. When AWDTSG posts are shared, screenshotted, and monetized through engagement, this statute may provide additional legal tools.
