    Idaho Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Idaho provides solid legal protections against defamation, with statutes that specifically address both civil and criminal dimensions. The FTC's Bureau of Consumer Protection provides additional resources for consumers facing online fraud and privacy violations.

    Idaho Code § 6-702 through § 6-715 governs defamation in Idaho. The state recognizes both libel (Idaho Code § 6-702) and slander. Libel is defined as a false and unprivileged publication that exposes a person to hatred, contempt, ridicule, or obloquy, or causes them to be shunned or avoided. AWDTSG Facebook posts clearly constitute libel.

    Idaho recognizes defamation per se for false statements that:

  • Charge criminal conduct
  • Impute a loathsome disease
  • Injure someone in their business, trade, or profession
  • Impute unchastity or serious sexual misconduct

    • Under per se treatment, general damages are presumed. The false accusations typical of AWDTSG posts — cheating, abuse, predatory behavior — routinely qualify. According to Pew Research Center, 41% of Americans have personally experienced some form of online harassment.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Idaho is two years under Idaho Code § 5-219(5).

    Idaho Code § 18-4801 through § 18-4809 addresses criminal libel in Idaho. The state retains an active criminal libel statute, and while prosecutions are rare, the criminal dimension adds weight to formal removal demands. Idaho Code § 18-7906 covers harassment, including electronic harassment through social media platforms. Targeted AWDTSG campaigns may constitute harassment under Idaho law.

    Idaho courts also recognize invasion of privacy claims, including false light and public disclosure of private facts, providing additional legal tools for AWDTSG post removal.
