    Pennsylvania Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Pennsylvania provides solid legal protections against defamation under common law principles, with some of the most well-developed case law in the country.

    Key aspects of Pennsylvania defamation law for Philadelphia residents:

  • Defamation per se in Pennsylvania covers false statements imputing criminal conduct, a loathsome disease, harm to business, trade, or profession, or serious sexual misconduct — no special damages required
  • Pennsylvania's statute of limitations for defamation is one year from publication
  • Pennsylvania courts have recognized internet publications, including social media posts, as satisfying the publication requirement
  • General and special damages are both available, with general damages presumed in per se cases
  • Pennsylvania does not have an anti-SLAPP law, removing a procedural barrier that exists in some other states
