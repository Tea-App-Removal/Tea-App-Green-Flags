    Florida Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Florida provides solid legal protections against defamation. Under Florida Statute § 770, defamation includes both libel (written) and slander (spoken), with specific provisions that apply to online publications like Facebook group posts.

    Key aspects of Florida defamation law for Miami residents:

  • Defamation per se in Florida covers false accusations of criminal conduct, having a loathsome disease, adultery or sexual misconduct, and statements that harm someone in their trade or profession — special damages don't need to be proven
  • Florida's statute of limitations for defamation is two years from the date of publication — longer than most states
  • Florida Statute § 836.04 makes certain defamatory statements a first-degree misdemeanor, adding criminal liability as potential leverage
  • Punitive damages are available in Florida defamation cases when actual malice can be demonstrated
  • Section 230 does not protect the individual poster in Miami — only the platform

    • Florida's two-year statute of limitations and the availability of punitive damages give professional removal services particularly strong leverage when pursuing removal on behalf of Miami clients.
