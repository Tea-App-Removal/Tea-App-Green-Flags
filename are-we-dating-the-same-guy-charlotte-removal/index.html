    North Carolina Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    North Carolina defamation law provides solid protections for Charlotte residents facing false AWDTSG posts. Under North Carolina common law, defamation requires proving a false and defamatory statement was published to a third party, causing damage to reputation.

    North Carolina recognizes defamation per se in four categories: accusations of criminal conduct, allegations of a loathsome disease, statements that injure someone in their profession, and accusations of serious sexual misconduct. False Charlotte AWDTSG posts frequently fall into multiple per se categories, meaning damages are presumed without requiring proof of specific financial losses.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in North Carolina is one year from publication. Given the speed at which Charlotte AWDTSG posts spread through the city's concentrated social networks, every week of delay significantly compounds the difficulty and scope of removal.

    North Carolina courts have also recognized claims for intentional infliction of emotional distress in cases involving online defamation.
