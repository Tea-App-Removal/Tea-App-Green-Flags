    Kentucky Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Kentucky provides strong defamation protections with both statutory provisions and well-developed case law. The FTC's Bureau of Consumer Protection provides additional resources for consumers facing online fraud and privacy violations.

    Kentucky Revised Statutes § 411.051 through § 411.061 governs defamation in Kentucky. The state recognizes both libel and slander, with AWDTSG Facebook posts constituting libel.

    Kentucky recognizes defamation per se for false statements that:

  • Impute criminal conduct
  • Impute a loathsome disease
  • Injure someone in their business, trade, or profession
  • Impute serious sexual misconduct or unchastity

    • Under per se treatment, general damages are presumed without requiring proof of specific financial loss.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Kentucky is one year under KRS § 413.140(1)(d). This is one of the shortest in the country, making immediate action essential for Kentucky AWDTSG victims.

    KRS § 525.080 addresses harassment in Kentucky, including communications through electronic means. Targeted AWDTSG posting campaigns may constitute harassment under this statute. KRS § 508.130 through § 508.150 covers stalking, including electronic monitoring and communication. Persistent AWDTSG targeting can constitute stalking under Kentucky law.

    Kentucky's right of publicity law (KRS § 391.170) protects against unauthorized use of a person's name or likeness for commercial advantage. While AWDTSG posts aren't overtly commercial, the statute may provide additional leverage in certain circumstances.
