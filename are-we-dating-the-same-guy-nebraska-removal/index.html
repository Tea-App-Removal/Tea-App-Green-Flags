    Nebraska Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Nebraska's legal framework provides meaningful protections for individuals falsely accused in AWDTSG groups.

    Nebraska common law defamation requires proving a false statement of fact, publication to third parties, fault (negligence for private figures), and resulting damages. False accusations posted in AWDTSG groups — visible to thousands of members — clearly satisfy the publication element, and the written nature of Facebook posts constitutes libel rather than slander. Neb. Rev. Stat. § 20-202 establishes Nebraska's civil rights framework that supports privacy-based claims. Combined with common law privacy torts recognized by Nebraska courts, this provides additional legal avenues beyond traditional defamation. Defamation per se is recognized in Nebraska for statements that accuse someone of criminal conduct, impute a loathsome disease, affect someone in their business or profession, or impute serious sexual misconduct. Many AWDTSG posts contain exactly these types of accusations, meaning damages are presumed without proof of specific financial harm.

    Nebraska courts have awarded punitive damages in defamation cases demonstrating actual malice. Ex-partner revenge posts — a common origin for false AWDTSG content — frequently demonstrate the malice necessary for enhanced damages.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Nebraska is one year from publication (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 25-208). This shorter window makes swift action essential. Every week you delay narrows your legal options.
