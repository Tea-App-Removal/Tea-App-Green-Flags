Ohio Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Ohio provides robust legal protections against defamation. Under Ohio law, defamation requires a false statement of fact published to a third party that causes harm to your reputation. Ohio Revised Code § 2739.01 and related case law establish clear frameworks for pursuing defamation claims.

Ohio recognizes defamation per se for statements that impute criminal behavior, harm someone's professional reputation, or accuse someone of a "loathsome disease." Many false accusations in Cleveland's AWDTSG group — claims of abuse, harassment, predatory behavior, or dishonesty — fall squarely within these per se categories, meaning damages are presumed without proof of specific financial losses.

Ohio's one-year statute of limitations for defamation claims means timing matters.
