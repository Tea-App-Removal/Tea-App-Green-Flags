    Pennsylvania Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Pennsylvania defamation law provides strong protections for Pittsburgh residents facing false AWDTSG posts. Under Pennsylvania common law, defamation requires a false and unprivileged communication to a third party that tends to harm the reputation of the subject.

    Pennsylvania recognizes defamation per se for statements that charge criminal offense, impute a loathsome disease, tend to injure someone in their business or profession, or impute serious sexual misconduct. Most false Pittsburgh AWDTSG posts contain accusations falling into these per se categories, meaning damages are presumed.

    Pennsylvania's statute of limitations for defamation is one year from publication. Pittsburgh's tight social networks mean posts spread quickly, making early action essential for both legal viability and practical damage control.

    Pennsylvania courts have also recognized invasion of privacy claims including false light, which can apply when Pittsburgh AWDTSG posts present someone in a misleading way even if individual statements contain some truth.
