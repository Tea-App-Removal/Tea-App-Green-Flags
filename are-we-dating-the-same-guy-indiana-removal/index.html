    Indiana Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Indiana provides clear legal protections against defamation with well-established statutory and case law frameworks.

    Indiana Code § 34-15-1-1 through § 34-15-1-4 governs defamation in the state. Indiana recognizes both libel and slander, with social media posts in AWDTSG groups constituting libel.

    Indiana recognizes defamation per se for false statements that:

  • Impute criminal conduct
  • Impute a loathsome disease
  • Tend to injure someone in their trade, business, or profession
  • Impute unchastity or sexual misconduct

    • Under per se treatment, damages are presumed without requiring proof of specific financial harm. Most AWDTSG posts qualify because they typically contain false accusations of cheating, abuse, or criminal behavior.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Indiana is two years under Indiana Code § 34-11-2-4.

    Indiana Code § 35-45-2-2 addresses harassment, including electronic harassment through social media. Indiana's harassment statute is broad enough to encompass targeted AWDTSG posting campaigns and coordinated online attacks. Indiana Code § 35-45-10 covers cyberstalking and the use of electronic communication to harass, intimidate, or threaten. Repeated AWDTSG posting or coordinated campaigns against an individual may constitute cyberstalking under Indiana law.

    Indiana courts have recognized invasion of privacy claims including false light and public disclosure of private facts. When AWDTSG posts reveal private dating history, personal information, or intimate details, these privacy torts provide additional legal leverage for removal.
