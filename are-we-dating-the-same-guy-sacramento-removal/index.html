California Defamation Laws and Your Rights

California provides some of the strongest defamation protections in the country. Under California Civil Code § 44-48, defamation encompasses both libel (written statements, including Facebook posts) and slander. California law allows plaintiffs to recover both general and special damages for defamatory statements.

California also recognizes defamation per se — when false statements accuse someone of criminal conduct, sexual misconduct, or behavior incompatible with their profession, damages are presumed without the need to prove specific financial losses. Many accusations posted in Sacramento's AWDTSG group qualify as defamation per se under California law.

Additionally, California's Comprehensive Computer Data Access and Fraud Act and various privacy statutes provide additional legal avenues for addressing online harassment.
