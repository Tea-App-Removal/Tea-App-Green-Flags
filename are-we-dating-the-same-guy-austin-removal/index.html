    Texas Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Texas provides strong legal protections against defamation under Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code Chapter 73. For Austin residents facing false AWDTSG posts, these protections create meaningful leverage for removal.

    Under Texas law, defamation requires proving a false statement of fact was published to a third party with at least negligence regarding the statement's truth or falsity. Texas recognizes defamation per se for statements that accuse someone of a crime, impute a loathsome disease, injure someone in their profession, or accuse someone of sexual misconduct. Most false AWDTSG posts in Austin fall squarely into these categories.

    Texas also has a one-year statute of limitations for defamation claims, so acting quickly on Austin AWDTSG posts is critical. Additionally, Texas courts have awarded significant damages in online defamation cases, which strengthens the legal leverage our removal notices carry.

    One important note: Texas has an anti-SLAPP statute (Texas Citizens Participation Act) that protects free speech on matters of public concern.
