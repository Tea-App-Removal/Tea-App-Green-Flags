The Reporting System Is Designed to Deflect, Not Resolve

Tea App's reporting mechanism was not designed to handle disputes about truth or falsity. The platform's categories focus on clear-cut policy violations, not on whether something is actually true. There is no option for "false information" or "defamation." The platform treats virtually any "experience" or "opinion" shared by a user as protected content.

The vast majority of reports never receive meaningful human review. The automated system filters for specific types of obvious violations, and a post making false claims about your character simply does not register as a problem in their system.

Multiple Reports Don't Help (They Hurt)

Many people think coordinating friends and family to mass-report a post will trigger removal. This strategy actually backfires. The platform's systems can detect coordinated reporting campaigns and may flag them as suspicious. In some cases, this can actually give the original post MORE visibility rather than less.

Evidence Doesn't Matter

You might have ironclad proof that a post about you is false—text messages, photos, witness statements, police reports, medical records. None of it matters to Tea App's reporting system. There's no mechanism to submit evidence. The platform doesn't adjudicate truth—they only enforce their community guidelines, which focus on explicit content, threats, and copyright issues, not factual accuracy.
