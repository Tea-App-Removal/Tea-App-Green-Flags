    Louisiana Civil Code Article 2315 provides the general framework for defamation as a tort, establishing that every act of a person that causes damage obligates the person by whose fault it occurred to repair it. This broad delictual framework covers defamation alongside other torts.

    Louisiana recognizes both defamation (the broad category) and distinguishes between statements that are defamatory per se and those requiring proof of special damages. Louisiana courts have recognized the following as per se defamatory:

  • Accusations of criminal conduct
  • Accusations of having a loathsome disease
  • Statements injurious to trade, business, or profession
  • Imputations of unchastity

    • Under Louisiana's per se doctrine, falsity, malice, and resulting injury are presumed.

    The prescriptive period (Louisiana's equivalent of a statute of limitations) for defamation is one year under Louisiana Civil Code Article 3492. This shorter timeline demands prompt action.
