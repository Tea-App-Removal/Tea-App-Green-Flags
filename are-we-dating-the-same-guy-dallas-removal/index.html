    Texas Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Texas provides meaningful legal protections against defamation. Under the Texas Citizens Participation Act (TCPA) and common law defamation standards, individuals can pursue action against those who publish false statements of fact.

    Key points about Texas defamation law for Dallas residents:

  • Defamation per se in Texas covers false accusations of criminal conduct, sexual misconduct, having a loathsome disease, and statements injurious to one's business or profession — no proof of special damages required
  • Texas statute of limitations for defamation is one year from publication
  • Texas courts recognize social media posts, including Facebook group posts, as sufficient publication for defamation claims
  • The TCPA (Texas anti-SLAPP statute) may come into play, but it protects against frivolous lawsuits — not against legitimate defamation claims based on provably false statements
  • Actual damages plus exemplary damages are available in Texas when the poster acted with malice
