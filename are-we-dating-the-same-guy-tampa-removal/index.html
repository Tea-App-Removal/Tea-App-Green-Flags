    Florida Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Florida's defamation laws provide meaningful protection for Tampa residents facing false AWDTSG posts. Under Florida Statute § 770.01, defamation encompasses false statements of fact published to third parties that damage reputation.

    Florida recognizes defamation per se for statements that accuse someone of committing a crime, having a contagious or loathsome disease, or impute conduct incompatible with the proper exercise of a lawful business or profession. False AWDTSG posts in Tampa that allege criminal behavior, sexual misconduct, or abusive patterns typically qualify as defamation per se under Florida law, meaning damages are presumed.

    Florida's statute of limitations for defamation is two years — more generous than many states — but this shouldn't encourage delay. The longer a Tampa AWDTSG post remains active, the more screenshots circulate and the more difficult comprehensive removal becomes.

    Florida also has a right of action for unauthorized publication of a person's name or likeness under Florida Statute § 540.08. When Tampa AWDTSG posts include your photographs without consent, this statute provides additional legal leverage for removal.
