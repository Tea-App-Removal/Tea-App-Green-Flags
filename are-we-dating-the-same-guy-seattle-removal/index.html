    Washington State Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Washington State provides meaningful legal protections against defamation under RCW 7.96 and common law principles.

    Key aspects of Washington defamation law for Seattle residents:

  • Defamation per se in Washington covers false statements imputing criminal conduct, a loathsome disease, sexual misconduct, or harm to business or profession — no special damages required
  • Washington's statute of limitations for defamation is two years from publication
  • Washington's anti-SLAPP statute (Uniform Public Expression Protection Act) provides some protections but does not shield provably false statements of fact
  • General and special damages are available, with general damages presumed in per se cases
  • Washington courts recognize online publication, including social media, as meeting defamation requirements
