    Arizona Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Arizona provides meaningful legal protections against defamation under A.R.S. § 12-651 through § 12-654 and common law principles.

    Key aspects of Arizona defamation law for Phoenix residents:

  • Defamation per se in Arizona covers false statements imputing criminal conduct, a loathsome disease, sexual misconduct, or harm to business or profession — no special damages required
  • Arizona's statute of limitations for defamation is one year from publication
  • Arizona's anti-SLAPP statute (A.R.S. § 12-752) provides some protection for speech on public issues, but does not shield provably false factual statements about private individuals
  • Actual and punitive damages are available in Arizona defamation cases
  • Arizona courts recognize online publication in Facebook groups as sufficient for defamation claims
