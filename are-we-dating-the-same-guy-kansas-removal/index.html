    Kansas Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Kansas provides solid defamation protections with a straightforward legal framework.

    Kansas Statutes Annotated § 60-515(1) sets the statute of limitations for defamation at two years from publication. Kansas follows common law defamation principles, requiring proof of a false statement of fact, publication, fault, and damages.

    Kansas recognizes defamation per se for false statements that:

  • Charge criminal conduct
  • Charge someone with a loathsome disease
  • Injure someone in their business, trade, or profession
  • Charge unchastity or serious sexual misconduct

    • Under per se treatment, general damages are presumed. AWDTSG posts containing false accusations of cheating, abuse, or criminal behavior qualify.

    Kansas Statutes § 21-6206 addresses criminal defamation in Kansas. The statute criminalizes knowingly false communications made with the intent to expose someone to public hatred, contempt, or ridicule. While criminal prosecution is rare, this statute adds weight to removal demands. K.S.A. § 21-6401 covers stalking, including electronic stalking behaviors. Targeted AWDTSG campaigns that involve repeated posting, monitoring, or harassing behavior may constitute stalking under Kansas law.

    Kansas courts recognize invasion of privacy claims, providing additional legal tools when AWDTSG posts share private information or portray someone in a false light.
