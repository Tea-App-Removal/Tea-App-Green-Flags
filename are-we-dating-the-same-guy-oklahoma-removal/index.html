    Oklahoma Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Oklahoma provides detailed statutory defamation protections that create clear legal leverage.

    12 O.S. § 1441 defines libel as a false and unprivileged publication by writing, printing, picture, effigy, or other fixed representation that exposes any person to public hatred, contempt, ridicule, or obloquy, or tends to deprive them of public confidence, or injures them in their occupation. Facebook AWDTSG posts fall squarely within this definition. 12 O.S. § 1442 defines slander and establishes the spoken counterpart to libel. While AWDTSG posts constitute libel, the per se categories inform damages analysis. 12 O.S. § 1443a establishes the framework for punitive damages in defamation cases, available when the defendant acted with actual malice. Revenge-motivated AWDTSG posts made by an ex-partner with knowledge of falsity frequently meet this standard. 12 O.S. § 1446 addresses retraction as potential mitigation but not a complete defense. Even if a poster deletes content after being confronted, liability for damages already caused persists.

    Oklahoma courts recognize defamation per se for statements imputing criminal conduct, loathsome disease, professional incompetence, or unchastity. These categories cover most false AWDTSG accusations and trigger presumed damages.

    Privacy torts are recognized in Oklahoma including public disclosure of private facts and false light publicity. AWDTSG posts sharing intimate personal details create additional causes of action.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Oklahoma is one year (12 O.S. § 95). This short window demands immediate action.
