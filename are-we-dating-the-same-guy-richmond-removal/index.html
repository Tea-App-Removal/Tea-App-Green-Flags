Virginia Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Virginia provides strong defamation protections under Virginia Code § 8.01-45 and related statutes. Virginia law recognizes both libel and slander and allows recovery of compensatory and punitive damages for false statements that damage reputation. Virginia courts have applied these principles to online and social media communications.

Virginia recognizes defamation per se for false statements imputing criminal conduct, a loathsome disease, unfitness for one's profession, or unchastity. AWDTSG posts in Richmond frequently contain accusations fitting these categories, meaning damages are presumed under Virginia law.

Virginia also has an insulting words statute (§ 8.01-45) that provides an additional legal avenue for addressing false accusations that damage reputation.
