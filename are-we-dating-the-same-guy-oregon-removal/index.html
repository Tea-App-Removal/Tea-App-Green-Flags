    Oregon Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Oregon provides strong legal protections through both defamation law and robust privacy frameworks.

    ORS § 30.170 establishes the right to bring civil actions for defamation in Oregon. This statutory foundation supports both libel and slander claims arising from false AWDTSG posts. Common law defamation in Oregon requires proof of a false statement of fact, publication to third parties, fault (negligence for private figures), and damages. Oregon courts have addressed internet defamation cases, establishing that social media posts — including Facebook group posts — constitute actionable libel. All AWDTSG posts fall under Facebook's Community Standards, including their Bullying and Harassment Policy. Defamation per se is recognized in Oregon for statements imputing criminal conduct, a loathsome disease, incompetence in one's profession, or unchastity. These categories trigger presumed damages and cover the most common types of false AWDTSG accusations. ORS § 163.732 addresses stalking, which may apply in extreme cases where AWDTSG posting is part of a broader harassment pattern. This criminal statute adds potential leverage for removal. Oregon's privacy protections are notably strong. The state recognizes invasion of privacy torts and has robust data protection frameworks. Public disclosure of private facts through AWDTSG posts creates additional legal avenues beyond traditional defamation.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Oregon is one year (ORS § 12.120). This tight window makes swift action essential for preserving legal rights while pursuing professional removal.
