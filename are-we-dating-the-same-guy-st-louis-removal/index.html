Missouri Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Missouri provides meaningful legal protections against defamation. Under Missouri law (RSMo § 509.050 and related case law), defamation occurs when someone publishes a false statement of fact to a third party that damages your reputation. Missouri recognizes both libel (written defamation, including social media posts) and slander.

Missouri also recognizes defamation per se — false statements imputing criminal conduct, professional incompetence, or a "loathsome disease" are presumed to cause damages. Many AWDTSG posts in St. Louis contain accusations that qualify as per se defamation, giving victims strong legal leverage without needing to prove specific dollar amounts in damages.
