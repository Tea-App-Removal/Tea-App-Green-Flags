Indiana Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Indiana provides clear legal protections against defamation. Under Indiana Code § 34-15-1-1 and related statutes, defamation occurs when someone publishes a false and defamatory statement about another person to a third party. Indiana courts recognize both libel (written defamation, including social media posts) and slander.

Indiana recognizes defamation per se for false statements imputing criminal conduct, a loathsome disease, serious misconduct in one's profession, or unchastity. Many accusations in Indianapolis's AWDTSG group fall within these per se categories, meaning you don't need to prove specific financial damages — the law presumes harm from the nature of the false statements themselves.
