    Illinois Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Illinois provides robust legal protections against defamation with some of the most plaintiff-friendly provisions in the Midwest. The FTC's Bureau of Consumer Protection provides additional resources for consumers facing online fraud and privacy violations.

    735 ILCS 5/13-201 sets the statute of limitations for defamation at one year from publication. This shorter timeline demands immediate action for Illinois AWDTSG victims.

    Illinois follows common law defamation principles as codified through extensive case law. The state recognizes both libel and slander, with AWDTSG Facebook posts constituting libel. Illinois requires proof of a false statement of fact, publication, fault, and damages (unless per se categories apply).

    Illinois recognizes defamation per se for false statements that:

  • Impute the commission of a criminal offense
  • Impute infection with a communicable disease
  • Impute inability or lack of integrity in the performance of employment duties
  • Impute a lack of ability or otherwise prejudice a person in their profession
  • Impute fornication or adultery

    • Illinois is notable for explicitly including adultery accusations in its per se categories — directly targeting the most common type of AWDTSG post. Under per se treatment, damages are presumed.
