Florida Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Florida provides strong legal protections against defamation. Under Florida Statute § 770.01-770.02, defamation encompasses false statements of fact published to third parties that damage your reputation. Florida courts have established clear precedents for both online and social media defamation cases.

Florida recognizes defamation per se for false statements that accuse someone of a crime, suggest someone carries a communicable disease, or harm someone's professional reputation. AWDTSG posts in Jacksonville frequently contain accusations that qualify as per se defamation under Florida law, meaning damages are presumed without requiring proof of specific financial harm.
