Utah Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Utah provides meaningful defamation protections under Utah Code § 45-2-1 through 45-2-15. Utah law defines defamation as a false statement of fact, published to third parties, that damages someone's reputation. Utah courts have applied these principles to online and social media communications.

Utah recognizes defamation per se for false statements imputing criminal behavior, a loathsome disease, incompetence in one's profession, or sexual misconduct. Many AWDTSG posts in the Salt Lake City group contain accusations that qualify as per se defamation under Utah law, meaning damages are presumed without proof of specific financial losses.

Utah also has a statute addressing cyber-harassment and electronic communications, providing additional legal tools for addressing online defamation.
