    Alabama Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Alabama provides some of the strongest legal protections against defamation in the Southeast. Understanding these laws gives you real leverage when pursuing post removal.

    Alabama Code § 6-5-180 through § 6-5-189 governs defamation actions in the state. Alabama recognizes both libel (written defamation, which includes social media posts) and slander. Under Alabama law, a defamation plaintiff must prove that the defendant published a false statement of fact about the plaintiff to a third party, and that the statement caused damage to the plaintiff's reputation.

    Alabama is particularly favorable for defamation victims because the state recognizes defamation per se — certain categories of false statements are considered so inherently damaging that you don't need to prove specific financial losses. These categories include false accusations of:

  • Criminal behavior
  • Sexual misconduct
  • Conduct incompatible with your business or profession
  • Having a loathsome disease

    • Many AWDTSG posts fall squarely into these per se categories, making Alabama an advantageous jurisdiction for pursuing removal through legal channels.

    Alabama Code § 13A-11-8 addresses criminal defamation, and while criminal prosecutions are rare, the existence of this statute adds additional legal weight to removal demands. Alabama courts have also recognized invasion of privacy claims under Alabama Code § 6-5-770, which can apply when AWDTSG posts expose private information without consent.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Alabama is two years from the date of publication under Alabama Code § 6-2-38. However, each new share or reposting of defamatory content can restart this clock, giving you extended legal options for removal.
