Tennessee Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Tennessee provides clear defamation protections. Under Tennessee Code § 29-24-103 and related statutes, defamation involves the publication of false statements of fact that damage someone's reputation. Tennessee courts have applied these principles to social media and online communications.

Tennessee recognizes defamation per se for false statements imputing criminal behavior, a loathsome disease, professional unfitness, or serious sexual misconduct. Many Memphis AWDTSG posts contain accusations that qualify under these categories, meaning damages are presumed without proof of specific financial harm.
