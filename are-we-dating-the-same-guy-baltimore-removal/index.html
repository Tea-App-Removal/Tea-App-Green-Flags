Maryland Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Maryland provides strong defamation protections. Under Maryland common law, defamation requires a false statement of fact, publication to third parties, fault on the part of the publisher, and resulting damage to reputation. Maryland courts have a well-developed body of case law applying these principles to online and social media communications.

Maryland recognizes defamation per se for false statements imputing criminal behavior, having a loathsome disease, adultery or unchastity, and unfitness for one's profession. Many Baltimore AWDTSG posts contain accusations that fall within these per se categories, meaning damages are legally presumed.

Maryland's proximity to DC also means federal legal frameworks can provide additional leverage in certain circumstances.
