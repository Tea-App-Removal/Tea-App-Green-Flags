Oklahoma Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Oklahoma provides robust defamation protections. Under Oklahoma Statute Title 12 § 1441-1450, defamation encompasses false statements published to third parties that injure reputation. Oklahoma law recognizes both libel and slander, and Oklahoma courts have applied these statutes to social media communications.

Oklahoma recognizes defamation per se for false statements imputing criminal conduct, professional incompetence, a loathsome disease, or unchastity. Many AWDTSG posts contain accusations that fall squarely within these categories. Under Oklahoma's per se framework, damages are presumed without the need to demonstrate specific financial losses.
