    Oregon Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Oregon defamation law provides clear protections for Portland residents facing false AWDTSG posts. Under Oregon common law and ORS Chapter 31 (governing defamation actions), defamation requires proving a false statement of fact was communicated to a third party, causing damage to reputation.

    Oregon recognizes defamation per se for statements that accuse someone of criminal conduct, impute a loathsome disease, injure someone in their profession, or allege sexual misconduct. Most false Portland AWDTSG posts contain accusations that fall into these per se categories, meaning damages are presumed without proof of specific financial loss.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Oregon is one year from publication under ORS 12.110. Given how rapidly Portland AWDTSG posts circulate through the city's tight social networks, acting quickly is essential to both legal viability and practical removal success.

    Oregon also provides protections under its false light invasion of privacy tort. This applies when Portland AWDTSG posts present someone in a highly offensive false light — even if individual statements contain some truth but create a misleading overall impression.
