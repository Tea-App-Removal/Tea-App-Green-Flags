    Georgia Defamation Laws and Your Rights

    Georgia provides clear legal protections against defamation. Under O.C.G.A. § 51-5-1 through § 51-5-11, Georgia law defines and provides remedies for both libel and slander.

    Key aspects of Georgia defamation law for Atlanta residents:

  • Libel per se in Georgia includes false statements that tend to injure someone in their trade, occupation, or profession, or impute a crime punishable by imprisonment — no special damages required
  • Georgia's statute of limitations for defamation is one year from publication
  • Punitive damages are available in Georgia defamation cases under O.C.G.A. § 51-12-5.1 when the defendant acted with willful misconduct, malice, or fraud
  • Georgia courts have recognized online publication, including social media posts, as meeting the publication requirement
  • Georgia does not have an anti-SLAPP statute, meaning there are fewer procedural hurdles to pursuing defamation claims
