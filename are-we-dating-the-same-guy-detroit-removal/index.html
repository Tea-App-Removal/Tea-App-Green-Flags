Michigan Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Michigan provides strong defamation protections. Under Michigan common law and the Michigan Penal Code, defamation occurs when someone publishes a false statement of fact that damages another person's reputation. Michigan courts recognize both libel and slander and have applied these principles to social media content.

Michigan recognizes defamation per se for false statements imputing criminal conduct, a loathsome disease, professional incompetence, or unchastity. Many AWDTSG posts in Detroit's group contain accusations that qualify as per se defamation, meaning damages are legally presumed without the need for specific proof of financial loss.
