Wisconsin Defamation Laws and Your Rights

Wisconsin takes defamation seriously. Under Wisconsin law, defamation occurs when someone publishes a false statement of fact that harms your reputation. Wisconsin Statute § 895.05 specifically addresses defamation and provides for both compensatory and punitive damages when false statements are made with actual malice.

Wisconsin is also notable for its recognition of defamation per se. Certain categories of false statements — including accusations of criminal behavior, sexual misconduct, or conduct that could harm your professional reputation — are considered so inherently damaging that you don't need to prove specific financial losses. Many AWDTSG posts in Milwaukee fall squarely into these categories, making them actionable under Wisconsin law.
