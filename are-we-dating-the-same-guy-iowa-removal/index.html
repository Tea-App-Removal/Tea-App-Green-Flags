    Iowa Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Iowa provides comprehensive defamation protections with a well-established legal framework.

    Iowa Code § 659.1 through § 659.4 governs defamation in the state. Iowa recognizes both libel and slander, with social media posts constituting libel. The state requires proof of a false statement of fact, publication, fault, and damages.

    Iowa recognizes defamation per se for false statements that:

  • Impute criminal conduct
  • Impute a loathsome disease
  • Impute unchastity or sexual misconduct
  • Injure someone in their business, profession, or occupation

    • Under per se treatment, damages are presumed. Most AWDTSG posts qualify because they routinely accuse men of cheating, abuse, or predatory behavior.

    The statute of limitations for defamation in Iowa is two years under Iowa Code § 614.1(2).

    Iowa Code § 708.7 addresses harassment, including electronic harassment through social media platforms. Targeted AWDTSG posting campaigns may constitute harassment under Iowa law.

    Iowa courts recognize invasion of privacy claims, including false light and public disclosure of private facts. These causes of action provide additional legal tools when AWDTSG posts reveal private information or portray someone in a misleading light.

    Iowa Code § 622.31 provides a privilege for certain communications, but false statements of fact in AWDTSG groups do not qualify for any protected privilege.
