    Mississippi Defamation and Privacy Laws

    Mississippi provides clear legal protections against defamation with both civil and criminal provisions. The FTC's Bureau of Consumer Protection provides additional resources for consumers facing online fraud and privacy violations.

    Mississippi Code § 15-1-35 sets the statute of limitations for defamation at one year from publication. This shorter timeline requires immediate action.

    Mississippi follows common law defamation principles, recognizing both libel and slander. AWDTSG Facebook posts constitute libel. The state requires proof of a false statement of fact, publication, fault, and damages (unless per se categories apply).

    Mississippi recognizes defamation per se for false statements that:

  • Impute a criminal offense
  • Impute a loathsome disease
  • Injure someone in their trade, business, or profession
  • Impute unchastity or serious sexual misconduct

    • Under per se treatment, general damages are presumed.

    Mississippi Code § 97-3-107 addresses cyberstalking, making it a crime to use electronic communication to threaten, harass, or intimidate. Coordinated AWDTSG campaigns may trigger this statute. Mississippi Code § 97-29-41 covers criminal defamation in Mississippi. The state retains provisions addressing malicious publication of false statements intended to injure reputation.

    Mississippi courts recognize invasion of privacy claims, providing additional legal tools when AWDTSG posts expose private information or create false impressions.
